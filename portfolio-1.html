Soil Analysis

This project involved providing a comprehensive solution for a consulting client to better manage and visualize their soil analysis results. The initial phase focused on data management, where I performed Extract, Transform, Load (ETL) operations to process raw soil analysis data and integrate it into a robust geodatabase.

Following data preparation, I moved on to data visualization. I developed an interactive web map using Leaflet JS. This map allowed the client to dynamically explore their soil analysis data, providing a clear and intuitive understanding of spatial patterns and trends.

A key enhancement to the interactive map is the integration of Chart.js graphs. These charts are dynamically linked to the map layers, providing additional analytical depth. Specifically, the graphs visualize:

  • The share of land for each type of soil, offering a quick overview of soil composition across the surveyed area.
  • The distribution of nutrients and micronutrients, providing insights into soil fertility.
  • The suitability of the land for agriculture based on the soil analysis, directly supporting agricultural planning and decision-making.

A key benefit of this solution was its accessibility. Since the client lacked a dedicated enterprise GIS (Geographic Information System), the web-based interactive map provided a powerful alternative. It empowered them to visualize and analyze their data independently, driving business decisions with greater ease and efficiency without the need for expensive software licenses or specialized training.
